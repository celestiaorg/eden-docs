## Vaults explained

A vault is a smart contract that pools user deposits and runs a predefined strategy such as lending, staking, or providing liquidity. When users deposit an asset, the vault issues shares that represent ownership in the pool. As the underlying strategy earns yield, the total pool grows and each share becomes worth more.

A good real-world analogy is a mutual fund or ETF. Investors pool capital, the fund executes a strategy, and profits flow back in. The difference is that a vault’s rules are enforced by smart contracts, not fund managers, and everything is onchain and fully transparent.

### Why use vaults

When you build vaults, you tap into [~$100B in onchain assets](https://defillama.com/) and a fast-growing tokenized RWA (Real World Assets) segment. You get access to real capital your product can reach on day one.

- **There’s real liquidity to tap.** [DeFi holds well over **$100B** in assets across protocols in 2025](https://defillama.com/). Vaults give users a single asset to hold while you route that liquidity into strategies.

- **RWAs are a tailwind.** Real-World Assets (RWAs) are expanding fast, [with over $24 billion now tokenized onchain](https://cointelegraph.com/news/private-credit-powers-24b-tokenization-market-ethereum-still-dominates-redstone) across Treasuries, private credit, and other yield-bearing instruments. For builders, RWAs open up access to stable, low-volatility yield sources that can be wrapped by vaults and composed directly into DeFi strategies.

- **Standards reduce integration cost.** An open vault standard means one set of deposit/withdraw semantics, one share model, and predictable previews. It also makes your product easier to list, monitor, and compose with other apps because everyone "speaks" the same interface.

- **Composability expands distribution.** Other apps can treat vault shares as building blocks (hold them as collateral, bundle them, or route capital into them) so your strategy can plug into a larger ecosystem day one.

### Configuring your vault

Before users can deposit, your vault needs two critical configurations:

1. **Supply Cap** - Maximum amount of assets the vault can hold (risk management)
2. **Supply Queue** - Ordered list of markets where deposits will be allocated

<ConfigureVault />

**Understanding Supply Caps**

Supply caps protect depositors by limiting exposure to any single market. As the vault curator, you set caps based on your risk assessment. Higher caps = higher risk but potentially higher yield.

The configuration process has two steps:

1. **Submit Cap**: Propose a new cap (subject to timelock)
2. **Accept Cap**: Accept the pending cap (after timelock expires)

Since we set timelock=0 for testing, you can submit and accept immediately.

**Understanding Supply Queue**

The **supply queue** is an ordered list of markets. When users deposit, the vault automatically:

1. Allocates funds to the first market in the queue (up to its cap)
2. If that market is full, moves to the next market
3. Continues until all funds are allocated or the queue is exhausted

For this tutorial, we're using a single market with these parameters:

- **Loan Token**: fakeUSD (what lenders supply)
- **Collateral Token**: fakeTIA (what borrowers provide)
- **LLTV**: 86% (loan-to-value ratio)
- **Oracle**: Mock oracle for testing

### Depositing to your vault

With fakeUSD tokens in your wallet, you're ready to deposit into your vault and receive vault shares in return.

The math used in vaults is simple, but it may not be what you expect as certain considerations are taken to reduce computation costs. You can dive deeper into this in the video below, or read on for details.

**INSERT LUKE'S VAULT MATH VIDEO HERE**

When you deposit into a vault, it mints you shares in exchange for your assets. The vault doesn’t track everyone’s balances individually, that would require looping over all users, which is gas-inefficient in Solidity. Instead, it just tracks two numbers:

- **`totalAssets`**: Total assets controlled by the vault
- **`totalSupply`**: Total shares issued to all depositors

The price per share then is calculated as `totalAssets / totalSupply`.

**Example**

Ten people each deposit 100 USDC, so the vault has 1,000 USDC total. The vault then mints 1,000 shares.

```
totalAssets = 1,000
totalSupply = 1,000
pricePerShare = 1
```

The vault buys a condo for that $1,000 and rents it out online. After a month, it earns $100 from rent, which is fed back into the vault, increasing `pricePerShare`.

```
totalAssets = 1,100
totalSupply = 1,000
pricePerShare = 1.1
```

Now, your original 100 shares are now worth 110 USDC, instead of the 100 USDC you deposited.

The vault never updated your balance, it just updated `totalAssets`.
That’s why vault accounting is simple and gas-efficient: two variables instead of thousands of storage writes.

**ERC-4626 Deposit Mechanics**

When you deposit assets into an ERC-4626 vault, here's what happens:

1. **Approval**: First, you approve the vault to spend your fakeUSD
2. **Deposit**: The vault pulls your fakeUSD and mints you shares
3. **Shares**: Shares are minted and transferred to your wallet, your shares represent ownership in the vault's total assets

**Key Metrics explained**

- **Total Assets**: Total fakeUSD controlled by the vault (increases as interest accrues)
- **Total Supply**: Total vault shares issued to all depositors (only changes on deposit/withdraw)
- **Share Price**: Current value of each share (totalAssets / totalSupply)
- **Your Shares**: Your portion of the vault, and its current value in fakeUSD

**How yield accrues**

Your vault earns yield through the underlying Morpho Blue market:

1. **Lenders supply** fakeUSD to the market
2. **Borrowers borrow** fakeUSD by providing fakeTIA collateral
3. **Interest accrues** on borrowed amounts
4. **Interest flows back** to lenders (including your vault)
5. **Share price increases** as totalAssets grows

The beauty of ERC-4626: **you don't need to do anything**. Just hold your shares, and they automatically appreciate as the vault earns yield.

### How prices work (oracles)

Every vault needs reliable asset prices to know how much its collateral is worth. But smart contracts can’t just look up prices from the web the way a normal app can. On a blockchain, every node must reach the same result from the same inputs, that agreement is called consensus.

If one node pulled a price from one website and another node saw a slightly different number, consensus would break and the network couldn’t agree on state. To prevent that, all data a contract uses must already exist onchain, where every node can read the same value.

That’s why vaults rely on oracles, which are special contracts that publish verified price data onchain so every node (and every vault), can reach the same result through consensus.

**How This Affects Your Vault**

Price changes impact the market in real-time:

- **Collateral Value**: If fakeTIA price drops, borrowers may face liquidation
- **Borrowing Power**: Higher prices mean borrowers can borrow more
- **Vault Health**: The vault monitors these prices to manage risk

In production, you'd use real oracles like **Chainlink** or **Pyth** that fetch actual market prices from exchanges. For testing, this mock oracle lets you simulate different market conditions.

## Troubleshooting

### Common issues

**"Wrong network" error**

- Ensure your wallet is connected to Eden Testnet (Chain ID: 3735928814)
- Add the network manually if it's not in your wallet

**"Insufficient funds" when deploying**

- Get more testnet TIA from the [faucet](https://faucet-eden-testnet.binarybuilders.services)
- Each transaction requires a small amount of gas

**"Insufficient allowance" when depositing**

- Click the "Approve" button before "Deposit"
- Wait for the approval transaction to confirm
- Then retry the deposit

**"Cooldown active" when minting tokens**

- The faucet has a 60-second cooldown per address per token
- Wait for the cooldown to expire, or use a different wallet

**Vault metrics showing 0**

- Make sure you've completed all previous steps
- Check that transactions have confirmed (view on explorer)
- Refresh the page if metrics don't update
