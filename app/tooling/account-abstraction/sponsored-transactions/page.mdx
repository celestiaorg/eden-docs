# Sponsored transactions (gas sponsorship)

import { Callout } from 'nextra/components'

“Gasless” doesn’t mean _free_, it means your user doesn't have to figure out how to have TIA before they even understand they should have a wallet. With smart account (ERC-4337), a **paymaster** can sponsor a user’s gas so the flow feels like a Web2 checkout with “free shipping.” This is how you turn “I don’t have tokens” into “One click sign up.”

## Why sponsor gas?

- Remove the “go buy native tokens first” blocker when onboarding new users.
- Let long flows (mints, swaps, quests) execute in one confirmation instead of prompting for each step.
- Communicate clear pricing: “Free”, “0.50 USDC”, or “covered by Eden” instead of fluctuating base fees.

## How ERC-4337 sponsorship works

1. User signs a **UserOperation** (no native tokens required).
2. The application includes **paymaster data** proving that someone will cover the gas.
3. Bundler submits the operation to EntryPoint.
4. During `validatePaymasterUserOp` and `postOp`, the paymaster proves eligibility and settles the gas cost (either from its deposit or by collecting a token reimbursement).

Think of a paymaster as the smart account’s “company card.” You set the rules; the bundler deducts from your balance when ops go through.

## Paymaster options you’ll see today

- **Verifying paymaster (a.k.a. sponsored gas)**
  Your backend or policy decides _which_ calls you’ll pay for (e.g., free mint, first swap, weekly cap). Users sign a UserOperation; the paymaster validates it and the bundler pulls fees from the paymaster’s deposit.

- **Token paymaster (“stablecoin gas”)**
  Users pay fees in a token they already hold (USDC, in-game token). The paymaster fronts the native gas, then collects reimbursement in that token during `postOp`. UX: show the token fee, not the network coin.

Both models are available through ZeroDev today: you configure policies in the dashboard, and the SDK handles the UserOperation wiring for you.

## Patterns for a great UX

- **No prerequisite funding:** users don’t need the native coin to start.
- **Predictable costs:** show “Free” or a fixed token price up front; hide volatile base fees.
- **Fewer prompts:** batch the flow, sponsor once, and let the operation run end-to-end.
- **Graceful fallback:** if sponsorship is unavailable, surface a helpful prompt (“pay 0.02 EDEN” or “try again later”).

## Designing your sponsorship policy

- **Eligibility:** which contracts/functions qualify? Are there per-user or per-app quotas? ZeroDev policies let you filter by target address, calldata, and more.
- **Risk controls:** rate limits, allowlists/deny lists, daily budget caps. Combine on-chain policy checks with backend analytics.
- **Funding plan:** keep the verifying paymaster deposit topped up; simulate expected burn before launch.
- **Observability:** track pending/included statuses, alert on stuck ops, and log every sponsored action for audits.

### Quick checklist (prod-minded)

- Define **eligibility**: which contracts/functions are sponsored, and when.
- Add **caps & rate limits**: per user, per day, per action.
- Provide a **fallback**: if sponsorship is exhausted, switch to token gas or prompt for native.
- **Observe** the lifecycle: surface “submitted → included” statuses; alert on stuck ops.
- **Fund & monitor** the paymaster deposit; set alerts for low balance.
- Document the user-facing messaging (“Gas covered by Eden” vs. “You’ll pay 0.25 USDC”).

<Callout type="warning">
  Avoid “Sponsor all transactions” in production. Scope sponsorship to specific actions and add
  per-user limits to prevent abuse.
</Callout>
